# Day 1 - CRM Basics

## 1. What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.

It is a system used by companies to manage customer information, communication, sales, and business processes in one place.

CRM helps organizations improve customer relationships and increase business efficiency.

---

## 2. Why Companies Use Salesforce

Companies use Salesforce because it helps them:

- Manage customer data easily
- Track sales and business activities
- Improve communication with customers
- Automate business processes
- Increase productivity and efficiency
- Store all customer information in a centralized system

Salesforce is one of the most popular cloud-based CRM platforms used worldwide.

---

## 3. Explanation of Salesforce Concepts

### Account
An Account represents an organization, company, or institution involved in business.

Example:
A college or university.

### Contact
A Contact represents a person associated with an account.

Example:
A student or parent.

### Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a possible business deal or process that may result in success.

Example:
A student admission process.

---

## 4. Business Flow

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

### Explanation

- Lead:
A student shows interest in joining the college.

- Contact:
The student's details are collected and verified.

- Opportunity:
The admission process begins for the student.

- Customer:
The student successfully gets admitted into the college.
